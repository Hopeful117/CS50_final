French Personal Pronouns

Person Singular Plural
1st Person je (I) nous (we)
2nd Person tu (you) vous (you, formal or plural)
3rd Person il (he/it), elle (she/it), on (one/we) ils (they, masculine or mixed), elles (they, feminine)

Examples of Usage

  1. Singular Pronouns
    • Je mange une pomme.(I eat an apple. -je refers to the speaker.)
    • Tu lis un livre.(You read a book. -tu refers to the person being addressed informally.)
    • Il joue au football.(He plays football. il refers to a male person or object.)
  2. Plural Pronouns
    • Nous allons au cinéma.(We go to the cinema. -nous includes the speaker and others.)
    • Vous travaillez beaucoup.(You work a lot.-vous can mean "you all" or a polite "you".)
    • Ils dansent bien.(They dance well. -ils refers to a group of males or a mixed group.)

Key points to remember

  1. "TU" vs "VOUS"
    • Tu: Used for informal conversations with friends,family, or someone close.
    • VOus: Used for formal situations, or when addressing more than one person.
  2. The pronoun "On"
    • "On" often replaces "nous" in informal French.
    • Example:On va au parc.(We are goins to the park.)
  3. Agreement with Gender and Number
    • Pronouns must agree with the gender (masculine/feminine) and number (singular/plural) of the noun they replace.
    • Example: Elles sont parties. (They left. – elles is feminine plural, so the verb agrees.)
